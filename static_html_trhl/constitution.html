A. Introduction

The Three Run Homer League (TRHL) is an e-mail/internet based simulation baseball league which uses Diamond Mind Baseball.

TRHL play began with the 1995 MLB season disk.

B. Philosophy

1. The TRHL is intended for the enjoyment of its members. Active participation via the web page and message board is strongly encouraged. Have fun.

2. All members must have purchased the following:

a) the Diamond Mind Baseball Game from Diamond Mind
b) the current year player ratings from Diamond Mind

Pirated copies are illegal.

3. The overriding rule of the TRHL is the honor system. Whether it is playing the games, managing your roster, dealing with other owners, or any other league-related activity, honesty is required.

4. There are no dues or fees to play in the TRHL. (All are encouraged, however, to show some signs of gratitude toward those volunteers that have made the TRHL the premier internet DMB league.)

5. League membership considerations:

a) If for any reason a manager should decide to leave the TRHL, please inform the League Office so the team can be offered to someone else.

b) If for any reason a temporary leave of absence is needed, please inform the League Office. Arrangements can usually be made.

c) Fanatical participation is encouraged (rants on the message board, two dozen trade offers per day, etc). Minimal participation is required.

Minimal participation is defined as:

1) missing no more than 4 home series in a season
2) missing no more than 2 home series before the all-star break
3) consistently voting on league matters

d. TRHL managers not adhering to 5.c.1-3 shall be subject to replacement.

e. TRHL managers not adhering to item B3 (the honor system), in the opinion of the league majority, shall be subject to expulsion and deep loathing.

 

C. Governance

1. League Commissioner

The league shall be run by the League Commissioner.

a. The League Commissioner (Commissioner) shall serve one year terms. Each term will begin immediately before the annual draft and end immediately prior to the next annual draft.

b. Prior to the expiration of the Commissioner's term, an election will be held to determine the next Commissioner. If the current Commissioner desires to serve an additional term and no one decides to run against him, no election is held.

c. The Commissioner has only the powers listed below:

1) appoint league officials and delegate powers and duties to them as he sees fit;

2) set all schedules and deadlines;

3) resolve rule disputes;

4) resolve rule ambiguities, including any situations that are not covered by the rules;

5) find and approve new team owners;

6) make minor rule changes, required for automation or streamlining of procedures, provided such changes do not change the spirit of the rules.

7) expel owners who do not comply with the rules.

d. The Commissioner has the following duties:

1) keep the league running in an efficient manner;

2) monitor voting for rule changes and Commissioner elections;

3) verify all team owners are using legal copies of DMB;

4) process each series file;

5) modify the Constitution, as necessary to incorporate all rule changes that pass.

2. Rule Changes

When a rule change is proposed, the Commissioner shall allow a sufficient time for discussion of the rule change. After that the Commissioner shall put the proposed change to a vote. If more than half the league votes for the change, then it passes and the Commissioner then modifies the Constitution to incorporate the change.

D. TRHL Rules

1. General

a) All rules are subject to interpretation by the TRHL Comissioner.

b) Constitutional revisions are subject to a league-wide vote.

2.Leagues

a) The THRL will have 2 leagues (National and American) each comprised of 3 divisions, with 4 teams in each division. This is subject to change as a result of future league expansion.

b) The AL will employ the designated hitter option while the NL will not.

c) The TRHL schedule will provide for limited interleague play. In interleague games, the DH rule will be used in AL parks and not used in NL parks.


3. Rosters

a) The total roster size is limited to 50 players. This applies from the beginning of the draft until the end of the playoffs. Off season rosters are unlimited in size.

b) From Opening Day to September 1 of a season , teams may only have 25 players on the active roster. The remaining players must either be on the disabled list or farmed (minor leagues).

c) On September 1 of a season, the active roster may be increased to 40.

d) Active postseason roster size is 25. This roster must be submitted by August 31st.

e) Sometime between the end of the TRHL World Series and the annual free agent draft (general target: January/February), teams must re-sign those players they will be retaining for the upcoming year. The web page will automate the process of re-signing players. This roster is known as the winter roster.

4. Player Salaries

a) The total team salary at any time between the Free Agent Draft and the end of the World Series must not exceed $400.

b) At the end of the World Series, each player's salary will be adjusted accordingly:

1) Current salary + 15% (round up all fractional remainders).
2) The minimum salary increase is $2.

Example 1) Craig Biggio's previous salary is $56. $56 + .15(56) = 64.4 = $65 (note roundup)
Example 2) Wendell Magee's previous salary is $4. $4 + .15(4) = 4.6 = $6 (note $2 minimum increase)

5. Free Agent Draft

a) At the beginning of each season, two weeks after the deadline for re-signing players, a auction-style draft will be held for available free agents. The free agent draft will be conducted over the TRHL Web page. This draft will continue until all players that have been nominated have been drafted and for 3 days no team chooses to nominate further players.

b) During the draft, salary cap and roster size restrictions must be met. No team may submit a bid which will violate rules C3a or C4a.

c) Any team (via the web page) may nominate any free agent not already nominated (subject to C5b) at any time.

d) Owners should recognize that around 5500 AB and 1300 IP will be needed over the course of a season. At a minimum, teams must come out of the draft with 4500 AB and 1000 IP.


6. Playing Time Limits

a) Two limitations on playing time will be enforced, and EXCEEDING either of them will require the player to be farmed for the remainder of the regular season:

1) a total limitation of 110%
2) a limit of 150% vs. LH or vs. RH

b) Rule C6a1 will apply to batters and pitchers alike.

c) Rule C6a2 only applies to batters for that "armed" pitcher for which they have the highest OPS (on-base % + slugging %) against.

Example 1) Todd Hollandsworth has an OPS of .800 in 20 AB vs LH pitching and a OPS of 750 in 300 ABs of RH pitching. He will be limited to 30 ABs vs LH pitching.

Example 2) Jermaine Allensworth has an OPS of .750 in 200 AB vs LH pitching and a OPS of 800 in 300 ABs of RH pitching. He will be limited to 450 ABs vs RH pitching.


d) In the event of a shortened season in which fewer than the normal 162 games are played, the TRHL will play a 162-game season and the player eligibility limits will be adjusted accordingly. These limits will be defined by the League Office in advance of the Annual Free Agent Draft.

e) League managers are encouraged to investigate and report potential violations of the usage rule. As with all TRHL matters, it is expected that honesty will prevail with occasional unintentional oversights.

f) In the playoffs, two limitations on playing time will be enforced, and EXCEEDING either of them in a single post-season series will require the player to be benched for the remainder of that series:

1) a total limitation of 8%
2) a limit of 12% vs. LH or vs. RH

g) Rule C5f1 will apply to batters and pitchers alike.

h) Rule C5f2 only applies to batters with less than 75 AB (vs. LH) or 300 AB (vs. RH) for that particular platoon split.

i) The limits in Rule C5f will be increased 1% for each six extra innings played.


7. Free Agents, Player Releases

a) Teams can release players at any time between the end of the draft and the end of the World Series. Such players become free agents upon their release and are immediately eligible to be signed by all teams except the player's original club. The original club must wait a one series period, before they can re-sign that player (presumably at a lower price).

b) Available free agents can be signed any time between the end of the draft and the end of the World Series. Acquisition of free agents is done via the web page. Teams will bid to acquire available free agents on a series by series basis, with the free agent going to the highest bidder.

c) In the event of a tie bid, the team with the current worst record will acquire the player.

d) The salary of the acquired free agent shall be the bid itself or the second highest bid plus $1, whichever is lower.

8. Trades

a) Trades can occur any time except between the period: August 1st (season time) and the end of the World Series.

b) Trades should be entered at the TRHL Web Trade Page.

c) All trades are subject to roster size and salary cap restrictions (except during the off season).

d) Roster cash cannot be exchanged as part of a trade.

e) Players to be named later may not be components of a trade.

9. Farm Team (Reserve Roster)

a) Players on a team roster but currently inactive comprise the farm team.

b) There is no limit on the amount of times a player can be farmed or promoted over the course of a season. However, on the third, and all subsequent times a player is farmed during a season, any other team may claim him at his current salary. For purposes of this rule, the first time a player is farmed does not count, if it is done prior to the start of the season, or if it is done immediately after he is acquired from the free agent pool.

c) Farm player's salaries do count against the salary cap.

10. Injuries

a) The TRHL will use the Actual option in DMB for generating injuries.

b) When injured a player may be put onto the Disabled List. His stay on the DL must be 15 days or the length of his injury, whichever is greater.

11.Stadiums

a) Teams are not permitted to change stadiums during the course of a season.

b) Home stadium dimensions can be altered in the off season, but must remain fixed from the beginning of the draft to the end of the World Series. All stadium changes should be announced to the league via the web page.

12.Playoffs

a) The three division winners make the playoffs as does one wild-card team (best overall winning % by non-division winner) from each league.

1) In the event of a tie for the division crown, a one game playoff will be played to decide the pennant.
2) Only eligible players as of the last day of the season are allowed to play.

b) The division winning team will be seeded 1-3 based on winning percentage and the wild card team will be seeded 4th. 1 plays 4 and 2 plays 3 in the first round games.

1) In the event of a tie, i) head to head records, (2 way ties only)
ii) division records,
iii) league records,
iv) coin toss
will be used in descending order to settle the seeding order.


c) All series are best of 7 in a 2-3-2 format, with the lower seeded team (1 is lowest) hosting the first 2 and last 2 games.

d) In the World Series, the team with the most intra-league wins will host games 1,2,6, & 7. In need of tiebreaker, a coin toss will determine home field.

 

E. Amendments

1. Long Term Contracts

A team may sign zero, one or two players to a long term contract. Contracted Players are retained for 2 years with no further salary increase. The contracted player may be released at any time, but the owning team will be penalized the amount of the contracted player's normal salary increase (only the increase, not the entire salary) for the remainder of the contract. 

Any team which signed two players to long term contracts in the preceding season, may not sign any in the current season.

Once the player's contract expires (third year with team), the player becomes a free agent. His team may not retain him.

 

2. Active Roster Minimum Playing Time

Players who did not attain 25 plate appearances, if they are a batter or 10 innings pitched, if they are a pitcher, may not appear on the major league roster of a team in the TRHL.

Players that do not meet these minimum requirements may be drafted, but they may not be promoted to a team's active roster.
